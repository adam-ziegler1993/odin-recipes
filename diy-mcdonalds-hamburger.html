DIY McDonald's Hamburger

A delicious DIY McDonald's Hamburger

Recreate the classic McDonald's Hamburger at home with this simple and easy-to-follow recipe. Enjoy the taste of McDonald's without leaving your kitchen!

A McDonald's Hamburger costs around $2. You can make this for about $1 and save money while enjoying a homemade burger!

Here's what you'll need to make 4 hamburgers:

  • 1lb Ground beef
  • 4 Hamburger buns
  • 4 Slices of American cheese
  • 1 Onion
  • Pickle slices
  • Ketchup
  • Mustard
  • Salt and pepper

Let's put it together:

  1. Form the ground beef into 4 equal patties and season with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat a skillet or grill over medium-high heat and cook the patties for about 3-4 minutes per side, until fully cooked.
  3. While the patties are cooking, slice the onion into thin rings.
  4. Toast the hamburger buns on the skillet or grill until lightly browned.
  5. Assemble the burgers by placing a patty on the bottom bun, followed by a slice of American cheese.
  6. Add pickle slices, onion rings, a squirt of ketchup, and a squirt of mustard.
  7. Place the top bun on the burger and serve immediately.
